Celebrations

Dandiya Night

The culmination of the First term couldn’t have been more lively and joyous. After the exams and their tedious preparation, the students and parents, were treated to an evening of fun and dance – the Dandiya Night. The evening saw a mesmerising surge of colour and gloss, with the radiant, traditional costumes adorned by the students, parents and teachers. Furthermore, the glitz was topped up with oodles of enthusiastic verve and energy exhibited by the dancers aged between 4 and 80. The evening was stirred into action by the teachers’ performances, shaken by the garba and dandiya prowess of the participants, and whipped into a frenzy by the scintillating music and the smooth symphony of dance and music. Prizes were given away to the most traditionally dressed boy, girl, family, the best dancing couple, most energetic dancer- boy, most energetic dancer-girl, the family with most number of participants and the oldest participant. The winners went home with the prizes, whereas the rest went back with prized memories of a fun-filled evening.



Independence day

Podar International School Bengaluru ICSE celebrated the 73rd Independence Day in true patriotic fervour. The National Flag was hoisted by Havaldar Dharma Prakash, APDC to mark a befitting start to the events lined up for the day. An array of cultural programs depicted the true Indian spirit of ‘Unity in Diversity’.



Practical Life Skills

Podar International School, Bengaluru ICSE organized Practical Life Skills sessions for Grade 1 to 9. This is the third year in a row that these sessions are being conducted. Children were informed in advance to bring the materials required for the specific activity wherever required. These practical sessions are aimed at making the students self-reliant for the day-to-day basic needs. Respective teachers imparted age-appropriate sessions in the classrooms, laboratories or inside the school premises. Students had hands-on experience in acquiring most of the skills. They were all excited to learn and master the new skills. Some of the activities include: making lemon juice, book/gift wrapping, changing bed linen, making a quick snack, removing stains from clothes, table etiquette, changing batteries of a clock and setting time, basic first-aid, some basic stitches, ironing clothes, changing bulb/tube light, packing suitcase, etc. The teachers conducted the session with a lot of enthusiasm and the students enjoyed each and every activity making it a very successful event.
